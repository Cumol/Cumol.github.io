Ly et al. 2018: Psychedelics Promote Structural and Functional Neural Plasticity.

31 Dec 2019 | categories: [ literature review ] tags: [ psychedelics lsd dmt psilocybin neuroplasticity ]

Note: This is my first attempt of a detailed literature review for “not-only” scientists. The idea is, instead of “dumbing down” science to a point it is far from true, I will attempt to explain as much as I see necessary. The hope is that the dissection of the articles into easily-understandable pieces will allow a deeper understanding of the subject. I will mainly focus on neuroscience/biology articles surrounding psychedelics, as this is a subject I, and many others, find interesting. Feel free to drop comments and criticism.

The first paper of this series is one published by Ly et al. (translated: and others) in 2018. As the study is an NIH-funded one, it can be publically accessible here. I will go through the figures one by one, building up to the whole story. I will use the public links to the paper, with the hope that this would keep me on the legal side of things.
